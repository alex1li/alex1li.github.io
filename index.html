Presidential Tweet Classifier

I worked with a close friend to develop a web application that uses machine learning to predict if a tweet from @realDonaldTrump is written by President Trump or a member of his staff. We collaborated with CS professors and graduate students in developing our data processing pipeline. Our latest system uses a gradient boosting algorithm from XGBoost, and achieved about 90% testing accuracy.
-
trumporstaff.com

Energy and the Environment Lab

On campus I am an undergraduate researcher in a lab focused on environmental sustainability. Previously I worked on a Python application that uses noise filtering and pattern recognition to determine pollution levels in wood burning emissions. I presented our lab's work at the 2017 American Association for Aerosol Research conference. That project sparked our lab's recent initiative, to develop IoT systems that can report real-time environmental data such as air pollution.

Cornell Rocketry Team

I've always loved rockets, and the Cornell Rocketry Team was an awesome way to gain experience with aerospace projects. I helped to design and fabricate a 15 ft launch vehicle to compete in a yearly competition sponsored by NASA. I managed the rocket's parachute recovery system, and built radios to track the rocket's speed, location, altitude, pitch througout flight.

Filmmaking

Every year I collaborate with a group of friends to write screenplays, shoot, film, and edit a short movie. We especially enjoy making films that convey a message, such as discussing the negative impact of human pollution or the dangers of texting while driving. In high school, entering these videos into short films contests was my number one source of income.
