About me

Hey, my name is Alex Li. I’m a junior at Cornell University majoring in Electrical and Computer Engineering and minoring in Business. I love watching movies, playing spikeball on the beach, and building things.

I am extremely interested in applying modern computational technology to solve big problems. I easily get interested in new topics, and I’m always looking to learn. Ultimately, I want to impact and improve lives through entrepreneurship.

Projects

Airframe and Communications Subteam

Cornell Rocketry Team

I've always loved rockets, and the Cornell Rocketry Team has been an awesome way to gain experience with aerospace projects. The team has competed yearly in a competition sponsored by NASA. On the airframe team I helped build a 15ft. tall launch vehicle that flew to over 5000 ft. I managed the recovery system, to ensure the rocket came back safely. On the communications team, I developed GPS radio beacons that tracked the rocket's location, speed, and altitude throughout flight, and transmitted the coordinates using a LoRa radio signal. The video helps shows tension and excitement of launch day.

Undergraduate Researcher

Energy and the Environment Lab

On campus I am an undergraduate researcher in a lab focused on environmental sustainability. Last year I worked on a Python application that uses noise filtering and pattern recognition to determine pollution levels in wood burning emissions. I presented our lab's work at the 2017 American Association for Aerosol Research conference. That project has sparked our lab's current initiative, to develop IoT systems that can report real-time environmental data such as air pollution. In making this data easily accessible, we can reduce the need for costly lab experiements.

Marketing Chair

CUABS has been one of the most influential organizations I've participated in at school. I've found mentors, friends, study-partners, family.
